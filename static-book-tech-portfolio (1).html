About Me

As a pioneer in the field of Digital Humanities, I explore the fascinating intersection of literature, technology, and data science. My research focuses on leveraging cutting-edge technologies to uncover new insights in literary analysis and to revolutionize how we interact with texts.

With a Ph.D. in Computational Linguistics from Stanford University and a background in Classical Literature, I bring a unique perspective to the evolving landscape of digital scholarship. My work has been recognized with the prestigious ACM Digital Humanities Innovation Award and has been featured in leading publications such as Nature Digital and the Journal of Digital Scholarship in the Humanities.

Currently, I lead the Digital Textual Analysis Lab at MIT, where we're developing groundbreaking tools for large-scale literary corpus analysis and innovative e-reading technologies.
